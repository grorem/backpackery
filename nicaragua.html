In early September, Tuesday the 3rd, I moved down to Managua, Nicaragua for work. I had a good idea of what it would be like, and in fact I had expected the heat and humidity, while still noticable, to be much worse. Despite most things feeling business as usual, there have still been some new, surprising things.

I jumped right into working the next morning after arriving, but it wasn't long until I finally got to get out and see some things. I've managed to get a few photos I hope you'll find interesting.

VOLCAN MASAYA

First real go-about in Nicaragua, and yes, it's active, and there are many other active volcanos in the area.

This entire area used to be one giant active caldera apparently.
GRANADA

I drove over to Granada for a bit of tourist wandering. Lots of bright, yellow Spanish colonial buildings.

Toña, one of the local beers, really pretty good - A cheapie but a goodie
San Juan del Sur

Really nice, albeit quite gringoed, beach area. Some sweet views
